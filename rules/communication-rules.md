# Communication Rules · Updated: 2026-06-21

Apply these rules for any stakeholder communication, meeting prep, or update drafting.

## Know Your Audience

Before drafting, confirm who the primary reader is and what decision or action they need.

- **CPO / Product Leadership:** strategic framing, OKR alignment, team output quality. Skip operational detail.
- **Engineering Lead / CTO:** cross-functional dependencies, delivery health, risk. Skip PM process detail.
- **Design Lead:** design quality, process consistency. Lead with outcomes, not steps.
- **Team members (PM Ops, Design Ops, TPM Ops):** process clarity, ownership, timelines. Be specific.

## Meeting Prep

- State the meeting purpose in one sentence.
- List: decisions needed (if any), topics for discussion (time-boxed), pre-reads required.
- For a 30-min meeting: max 2 decision items. For 60-min: max 3.

## Stakeholder Updates

- Lead with the so-what, not the detail.
- Format: **Status** (On track / At risk / Blocked) → Key change since last update → Next milestone → Ask (if any).
- Flag risks explicitly. Do not bury them in context.

## Confluence Publishing

- When a document is finished and share-worthy: offer to publish to the appropriate Confluence space as the default behaviour.
- A Confluence publish failure must: retain the draft locally, notify the user, never silently disappear.
